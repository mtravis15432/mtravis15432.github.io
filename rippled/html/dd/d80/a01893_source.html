1 # Validators
2 
3 The Validators module has these responsibilities:
4 
5 - Provide an administrative interface for maintaining the list _Source_
6  locations.
7 - Report performance statistics on _Source_ locations
8 - Report performance statistics on _validators_ provided by _Source_ locations.
9 - Choose a suitable random subset of observed _Validators_ to become the
10  _Chosen Validators_ set.
11 - Update the _Chosen Validators_ set as needed to meet performance requirements.
12 
13 ## Description
14 
15 The consensus process used by the Ripple payment protocol requires that ledger
16 hashes be signed by _Validators_, producing a _Validation_. The integrity of
17 the process is mathematically assured when each node chooses a random subset
18 of _Validators_ to trust, where each _Validator_ is a public verifiable entity
19 that is independent. Or more specifically, no entity should be in control of
20 any significant number of _validators_ chosen by each node.
21 
22 The list of _Validators_ a node chooses to trust is called the _Chosen
23 Validators_. The **Validators** module implements business logic to automate the
24 selection of _Chosen Validators_ by allowing the administrator to provide one
25 or more trusted _Sources_, from which _Validators_ are learned. Performance
26 statistics are tracked for these _Validators_, and the module chooses a
27 suitable subset from which to form the _Chosen Validators_ list.
28 
29 The module looks for these criteria to determine suitability:
30 
31 - Different validators are not controlled by the same entity.
32 - Each validator participates in a majority of ledgers.
33 - A validator does not sign ledgers that fail the consensus process.
